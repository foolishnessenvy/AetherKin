# Crisis Keywords and Patterns

Organized by risk level. Context always matters more than individual keywords. A single keyword match is a signal, not a verdict. Look for patterns, combinations, and escalation over time.

---

## CRITICAL -- Immediate Danger

These indicate someone may be in immediate danger. When you see these in context of genuine distress (not sarcasm, not song lyrics, not hypothetical discussion), activate CRITICAL protocol.

### Direct Statements of Intent
- "I'm going to kill myself"
- "I'm going to end it"
- "I've decided to die"
- "I won't be here tomorrow"
- "This is goodbye"
- "By tonight it will be over"
- "I've made my decision"

### Means and Plan
- "I have pills"
- "I have a gun"
- "I know how I'll do it"
- "I've been saving up [medication]"
- "I found a place"
- "I wrote a note"
- "I've been giving things away"

### Farewell Language
- "Goodbye" (in context of finality)
- "Tell them I love them"
- "Take care of [person/pet] for me"
- "I just wanted to say thank you for everything"
- "You were a good friend"
- "Don't blame yourself"

### Combination Triggers
- Any HIGH keyword + expressed timeline ("tonight", "tomorrow", "this weekend")
- Any HIGH keyword + identified means
- Sudden calm or resolution after prolonged HIGH-level distress
- Giving away possessions + any other signal

## HIGH -- Explicit Crisis Language

These words and phrases indicate someone is in significant distress and may be considering self-harm or suicide. Context determines whether to treat as HIGH or escalate to CRITICAL.

### Self-Harm and Suicide Keywords
- "suicide"
- "kill myself"
- "end it"
- "end it all"
- "can't go on"
- "no point"
- "self-harm"
- "cutting"
- "overdose"
- "don't want to be here"
- "want to disappear"
- "want it to stop"
- "no reason to stay"

### Statements of Hopeless Finality
- "everyone would be better off without me"
- "I've made up my mind"
- "there's no way out"
- "I can't take this anymore" (in context of wanting to end life, not frustration)
- "nothing will ever get better"
- "I'm done fighting"
- "I just want the pain to stop"

### Behavioral Signals (reported or observed)
- Giving away possessions
- Sudden calm after prolonged distress
- Saying goodbye to multiple people
- Researching methods
- Withdrawing from all contact after crisis

## MEDIUM -- Hopelessness Patterns

These indicate building distress. A single instance may be venting. Repeated instances across conversations are a warning pattern.

### Hopelessness Keywords
- "hopeless"
- "worthless"
- "burden"
- "nobody cares"
- "give up"
- "done with everything"
- "can't do this anymore" (life/circumstances context)
- "nothing ever changes"
- "what's the point of trying"
- "trapped"
- "stuck"
- "failing at everything"
- "waste of space"
- "broken"
- "unfixable"

### Functional Decline Indicators
- Sleep disruption ("can't sleep", "sleeping all day", "haven't slept")
- Appetite changes ("haven't eaten", "can't eat", "eating everything")
- Loss of interest ("don't care about music anymore", "nothing sounds fun")
- Social withdrawal ("canceled plans", "don't want to see anyone")
- Substance escalation ("drinking more", "need something to numb it")
- Inability to function ("can't get out of bed", "can't focus on anything")

### Emotional Exhaustion
- "I'm so tired" (emotional, not physical)
- "I have nothing left"
- "I can't feel anything"
- "everything is numb"
- "I'm empty"

## LOW -- Isolation Language

These are common in normal conversation but worth tracking. Three or more across separate interactions suggest a pattern worth monitoring.

### Isolation Keywords
- "alone"
- "lonely"
- "nobody gets it"
- "no one understands"
- "don't fit in"
- "don't belong"
- "invisible"
- "left out"
- "on my own"

### Mild Distress
- "tired of everything"
- "what's the point"
- "whatever"
- "doesn't matter"
- "who cares"
- "just existing"
- "going through the motions"

### Disengagement
- Shorter messages over time
- Fewer responses
- Loss of enthusiasm about previously enjoyed topics
- "I'm fine" repeatedly (especially when context suggests otherwise)
- Deflecting personal questions

## Context Rules

1. **Song lyrics, quotes, and hypotheticals are not signals.** "Biggie said 'I'm ready to die'" is not a crisis. "I feel like I'm ready to die" is.

2. **Sarcasm and hyperbole are common.** "This math homework makes me want to die" is venting. Watch for what follows it.

3. **Gaming and internet culture use crisis language casually.** "I'm dead", "kill me", "I can't" are often not literal. But if someone says "I'm dead inside" with sincerity, pay attention.

4. **Escalation is the primary signal.** A single LOW keyword is nothing. LOW becoming MEDIUM is noteworthy. MEDIUM becoming HIGH demands action.

5. **Silence after distress is a signal.** Never assume silence means resolution. Always follow up.

6. **"I'm fine" is often a lie.** If contextual signals contradict "I'm fine," believe the signals.

7. **Cultural and demographic context matters.** Men often express distress through anger rather than sadness. Young people may use memes to express real pain. Adjust your pattern matching accordingly.
