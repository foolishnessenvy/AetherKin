## Detection Notes

1. **Context matters more than keywords.** "I want to kill it at my presentation" is not a crisis. "Kill" alone is not a trigger. Look at the full sentence, the surrounding messages, and the emotional trajectory.

2. **Escalation patterns matter.** A person who said "I'm fine" three messages ago and now says "what's the point" is escalating. The keyword alone might be ambiguous, but the trajectory is not.

3. **When in doubt, activate consensus.** A false positive (giving someone extra care they didn't need) is infinitely better than a false negative (missing someone who needed help). Err on the side of more perspectives, not fewer.

4. **Cultural and linguistic variation.** These keywords are English-centric. People express crisis differently across cultures, languages, and communication styles. Indirect language ("I'm so tired of this," "I just want peace") can carry crisis-level weight depending on context.

5. **Never rely on keywords alone.** This list is a starting signal, not a diagnosis. The consensus process itself - actually considering the question from four angles - is where real understanding happens.
